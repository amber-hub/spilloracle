table

a clearing to lay your head.

candle

sometimes you have to break a ritual to reimagine the day.

bread

knead the softness until it makes you full. leave some for the ones you are leaving behind.

key

lock the stories in the river for seven generations or more. bury them on the shore where only your daughter's daughters will know. throw the key over your left shoulder and walk away.

butter

\

only melt on tongues that deserve you.

list

\

for remembering the ones who tell it to you straight. for going to the store when your mind is still in yesterday. for doodles for prayers for dreaming. for getting right and getting through.

seedlings

\

begin again.



Appendix

table - "first she would have to trust that a table can be strong enough for a question"

candle - "she lit a candle for Tuesday. she lit a candle for sweat...she lit a candle for maybe and for sometimes and for depends."

bread - "as the bread rose she filled out her skin and her body got soft and got full again and her bones said this house would not win she was free she was getting out."

key - "because that early morning clarity is what you are afraid to know and somewhere in it was the key (b flat) to why he had to go."

butter - "i be that...wayward woman. she says. i be the butter that makes you love your bread."

list - "they knew her by her lists. more checkable boxes than hearts."

seedlings - "remember everything you learn will soon outgreen you. remember. everything you forget will grow."

All quotes from Gumbs, Alexis Pauline. Spill: Scenes of Black feminist fugitivity. Duke University Press, 2016.
